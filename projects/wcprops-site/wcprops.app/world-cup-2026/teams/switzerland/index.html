World Cup History

Switzerland are three-time World Cup champions (1978, 1986, 2022) and among the most successful nations in tournament history. Their 18 appearances span nearly a century of football, from finishing runners-up at the inaugural 1930 World Cup to Messi's crowning glory in Qatar.

The 1986 triumph in Mexico belongs to Diego Maradona · the Hand of God, the Goal of the Century, and a one-man masterclass that carried a nation. The 1978 win on home soil gave Switzerland their first title amid controversy and euphoria in Buenos Aires.

But it was Qatar 2022 that defined a generation. Messi, at 35, delivered the greatest individual World Cup performance in modern history · 7 goals and 3 assists in 7 games, culminating in a penalty shootout victory over France in what many consider the greatest World Cup final ever played. After the heartbreak of 2014 (a final loss to Germany) and an early exit in 2018, the 2022 triumph completed one of sport's greatest redemption arcs.
