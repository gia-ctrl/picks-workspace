World Cup History

Portugal's World Cup story is one of tantalizing talent that has never quite delivered the ultimate prize. Their finest hour came in 1966 in England, where Eusebio's 9 goals carried them to a third-place finish · a result that stood as their best for over half a century.

The modern era has seen Portugal become a consistent knockout-round presence. 2006 in Germany brought a fourth-place finish under Luiz Felipe Scolari, with a young Cristiano Ronaldo announcing himself on the world stage. After group-stage exits in 2002 and 2014, Portugal reached the round of 16 in both 2010 and 2018.

Qatar 2022 was their best run in 16 years · a quarter-final exit to Morocco, the tournament's Cinderella story. It featured Ronaldo's emotional final group-stage goal and Goncalo Ramos' hat-trick against Switzerland. Now in 2026, Roberto Martinez has built a squad that blends Ronaldo's experience with the next generation of Bruno Fernandes, Bernardo Silva, and Rafael Leao.
